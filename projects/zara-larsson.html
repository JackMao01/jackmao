What if an album wasn't just heard — but read, felt, and lived on the page like a magazine cover story?

Album Editorial

Reimagining Zara Larsson's So Good not as a tracklist — but as a full editorial experience. Every page a spread. Every lyric a headline. Every photograph a mood board.

The Brief

So Good, released in March 2017, was Zara Larsson's global coming-of-age moment — a record that bridged Scandinavian pop sensibility with mainstream R&B and dance production. It debuted across charts in over 20 countries and cemented her as one of the most distinctive voices of her generation.

This project asked a single provocative question: if So Good were a magazine, what would it look like? The answer became a full editorial redesign — treating the album as both subject and structure, reimagining its energy through typography, photography direction, and layout design.

"She doesn't just make pop music. She makes pop culture — and this album is the proof."

— Editorial concept, Jack Mao / 2025
Design Direction

The magazine's visual identity draws from the album's own tension between softness and power. Pastel gradients are cut through by bold blackletter headlines. Intimate photography sits beside hard-edged typography. Nothing is entirely one thing — just like the record itself.

The colour palette pulls from the album's promotional materials: deep magentas, champagne golds, and cool lilacs — accented by stark white and near-black negative space.

Typography was treated as a design element in its own right. Display headlines were set oversized and cropped — partially bleeding off the page as if the ideas were too big to contain. Pull quotes were extracted from lyrics and given the weight of editorial copy.

Each section of the magazine corresponds to a sonic chapter of the album — the opener, the ballads, the anthems, the closer — each with its own visual register.

Typography & Layout

Headlines were set in an ultra-compressed serif with extreme tracking — evoking the loud confidence of Larsson's vocal delivery. Body copy uses a refined grotesque with generous leading, giving the pages room to breathe between the high-energy visual moments.

Layout was designed on a strict 12-column grid — but rules were broken deliberately. Full-bleed images interrupt the structure. Type runs over photography. Colour blocks shift from spread to spread, keeping the reader visually engaged from page one to the back cover.

Reflection

This project reinforced how music and editorial design share the same fundamental DNA — both are about pacing, tension, and release. An album builds from track to track; a magazine builds from spread to spread. The best version of both leaves you wanting to start again from the beginning.

Designing through the lens of Zara Larsson's So Good was an exercise in translating sonic energy into visual form — and a reminder that the most interesting design problems live at the edge of two disciplines.
