HiDesign —
Interface Study

FORM

A deep-dive UI exploration — designing an interface from first principles, questioning every convention, and rebuilding from the ground up.

Overview

HiDesign is a self-initiated interface study that challenges the conventions of modern UI design. Rather than following established patterns, the project asks: what would a digital interface look like if it were designed without reference to existing systems?

The result is a set of screens that feel simultaneously unfamiliar and intuitive — borrowing logic from physical objects, architectural space, and natural systems rather than digital precedent.

Process

The project began with two weeks of deliberately not opening Figma. Sketching, reading, looking at architecture and industrial design — building a visual vocabulary that wasn't rooted in digital UI.

Once that foundation was established, the translation into screen design happened quickly. The constraints of a digital canvas became interesting rather than limiting — every element had to earn its place on screen.

Reflection

The biggest lesson: familiarity is a design tool, not a constraint. The most successful screens in this study were the ones that borrowed just enough convention to feel navigable, then broke away sharply in one unexpected direction.
